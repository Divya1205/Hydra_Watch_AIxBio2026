PATHOGENS DETECTED IN WASTEWATER - CASPER STUDY
================================================
Source: "Deep untargeted wastewater metagenomic sequencing from sewersheds across the United States"
medRxiv preprint, March 2026


RESPIRATORY VIRUSES
-------------------
- SARS-CoV-2
  Subtype analysis: Yes, via Freyja (lineage deconvolution, PANGO nomenclature)
  Notes: Multiple Omicron subvariants tracked over time; new variants detected in wastewater earlier than in clinical reports

- Influenza A
  Subtype analysis: Yes, including seasonal subtypes (H1N1, H3N2) and highly pathogenic avian influenza H5N1
  Notes: H5N1 detected independently of clinical case reports; phylogenetically linked to North American outbreak strains

- Influenza B
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level only

- RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus)
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Strong correlation with clinical testing data (R up to 0.92)

- Human Coronavirus HCoV-HKU1
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level; clear seasonal patterns observed

- Human Coronavirus HCoV-NL63
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level; clear seasonal patterns observed

- Human Coronavirus HCoV-229E
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level; clear seasonal patterns observed

- Human Coronavirus HCoV-OC43
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level; clear seasonal patterns observed

- Parainfluenza 2
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level

- Parainfluenza 3
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level

- Human Metapneumovirus
  Subtype analysis: No
  Notes: Detected at species level

NORMALIZATION & QUALITY CONTROL
--------------------------------
- All pathogen abundances normalized to Pepper Mild Mottle Virus (PMMoV),
  a plant virus consistently present in human fecal matter
- Abundances expressed as fold change relative to site-specific median
- rRNA fraction used as sequencing quality control metric
